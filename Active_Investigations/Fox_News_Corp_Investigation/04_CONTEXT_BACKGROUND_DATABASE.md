## üèõÔ∏è FOUNDING & CORPORATE HISTORY

### Pre-Fox Corporation Era (1996-2019)

**1996: Fox News Channel Launch**
- Founded by Rupert Murdoch and Roger Ailes
- Part of News Corporation (later 21st Century Fox)
- Initial strategy: Conservative-leaning alternative to CNN/MSNBC
- Slogan: "Fair and Balanced" (until 2017)

**Key Growth Period (1996-2016):**
- Became #1 rated cable news network
- Held #1 position for 23 consecutive years (still ongoing)
- Built around primetime opinion/commentary model
- Strong viewer loyalty and demographic (older, conservative)

**2016: Roger Ailes Resignation**
- July 2016: Resigned amid sexual harassment allegations
- Died May 2017
- Suzanne Scott eventually becomes CEO (2018) - first woman

**2017-2018: Disney Acquisition Planned**
- Murdoch family decides to sell most of 21st Century Fox to Disney
- Rationale: Streaming competition, changing media landscape
- Assets to keep: News and sports properties
- Assets to sell: Entertainment content, FX, film studio, etc.

**March 19, 2019: Fox Corporation Created**
- **The Transaction:** 21st Century Fox assets split
  - Entertainment ‚Üí Sold to Disney for $71.3 billion
  - News/Sports ‚Üí Became Fox Corporation (new standalone company)
- **Stock Structure:** Dual-class shares maintain Murdoch control
- **Result:** Fox Corp begins trading independently (NASDAQ: FOXA, FOX)

### Industry Competition (2025)

**Market Position Rankings (Average Daily Viewers):**
1. **Fox News Channel:** #1 (23rd consecutive year)
   - Typical: 2-3 million primetime viewers
   - Demographics: Older (median age ~68), conservative-leaning

   - Leadership: Multiple CEO changes post-2021
   - Demographics: Broader age range, center-left lean

3. **MSNBC:** #3
   - Owned by: NBCUniversal/Comcast
   - Demographics: Liberal-leaning audience
   
4. **Newsmax:** Emerging competitor
   - Ownership: Private (Christopher Ruddy)
   - Viewership: 150K-400K daily average
   - Position: Further right than Fox News
   - Distribution: Lost DirecTV 2022, regained later

5. **One America News (OAN):** Diminished competitor
   - Lost DirecTV 2022, Verizon distribution
   - Significantly reduced reach
   - Position: Far-right

**Business Model Context:**
- **Dual Revenue:** Affiliate fees (cable carriage) + advertising
- **Affiliate Fees:** Estimated $2-3 per subscriber per month (Fox News)
  - More valuable than advertising for cable news
  - Why cable/satellite distribution critical
- **Advertising:** Pharmaceutical, insurance, financial services dominate
- **Trend:** Cord-cutting threatens traditional model ‚Üí streaming pivot

## ‚öñÔ∏è LEGAL & REGULATORY CONTEXT

### Defamation Law Landscape

**Dominion Settlement Context ($787.5M, April 2023):**
- **Largest media defamation settlement in U.S. history**
- Previous record: ~$177M (various cases)
- **Standard:** "Actual malice" required for public figures
  - Knowledge of falsity OR reckless disregard for truth
- **Fox's Position:** Defended as opinion/commentary, reporting claims
- **Settlement:** Avoided trial, no admission of liability, but massive payout

**Smartmatic Lawsuit Context ($2.7B claim, Active):**
- **Higher Damages:** Claiming more extensive harm
- **Different Defendant:** Voting machine company, not governmental entity
- **Status:** Appeals court rejected dismissal (Jan 2025) - case proceeds
- **Industry Impact:** Chilling effect on election misinformation claims

**Historical Context:**
- Fox News rarely sued for defamation
- When sued, typically wins on First Amendment grounds
- These voting machine cases different: specific false factual claims alleged

### FCC Regulation Context

**Broadcast Licenses (Fox Television Stations):**
- FCC licenses must be renewed periodically
- License challenges historically rare but possible
- Public interest standard
- Fox stations: 29 owned-and-operated

**Cable Network (Fox News) Regulation:**
- NOT subject to FCC content regulation (cable, not broadcast)
- No Fairness Doctrine equivalent
- First Amendment protections very strong

## üåê INTERNATIONAL COMPARISON CONTEXT

**U.S. vs. Other Democracies:**
- **U.S. Model:** First Amendment = very strong press freedom, minimal content regulation
- **UK Model:** Ofcom regulates broadcast content, "due impartiality" requirements
  - Sky News (formerly Murdoch-owned) subject to UK rules
- **Australia Model:** Australian Communications and Media Authority oversight
- **Canada Model:** CRTC has content/balance requirements

**Result:** Fox News business model would not work in most other democracies due to content regulations.
