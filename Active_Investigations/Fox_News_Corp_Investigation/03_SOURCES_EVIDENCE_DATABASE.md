## üîç INVESTIGATIVE METHODOLOGY FOR SOURCES

### Source Hierarchy (Truth-Seeking Methodology)
1. **Tier 1 (Highest):** Federal legal filings (SEC, FEC, Court records)
   - Criminal penalties for fraud
   - Third-party auditing
   - Public accountability

2. **Tier 2 (High):** Corporate direct disclosures reviewed by board
   - Political Activities Reports
   - Proxy statements
   - 10-K risk factors

3. **Tier 3 (Medium-High):** Sworn testimony / Depositions
   - Court proceedings
   - Congressional testimony
   - Under penalty of perjury

4. **Tier 4 (Medium):** Corporate PR / Investor Relations
   - Press releases
   - Earnings calls
   - Self-promotional; verify material claims

5. **Tier 5 (Variable):** News reporting
   - Quality varies by outlet and reporter
   - Check reporter's track record
   - Verify against primary sources
   - Note potential conflicts (competitor coverage)

6. **Tier 6 (Low):** Social media / Blogs
   - May contain leads
   - Never cite without primary source verification
   - Useful for finding what to investigate, not as evidence

### Red Flags for Source Skepticism:
- üö© "According to sources familiar with..."
- üö© Competitor reporting on competitor
- üö© No citation to primary document
- üö© Passive voice hiding attribution ("It is believed that...")
- üö© Round numbers without source ($5 billion, $10 million, etc.)
- üö© Conflicting reports without explanation

### Verification Checklist:
- [ ] Can I access the primary document?
- [ ] Does the secondary source quote accurately?
- [ ] Are numbers exactly matching primary source?
- [ ] Is context provided or cherry-picked?
- [ ] Who benefits from this narrative?
- [ ] What's the counter-evidence?

## üìå INVESTIGATION WORKFLOW

### For Every New Claim:
1. **Receive information** (news article, tip, report)
2. **Ask:** What's the primary source?
3. **Locate primary source** (SEC filing, FEC, court doc)
4. **Verify exact language** (not paraphrased)
5. **Check context** (full document, not cherry-picked)
6. **Document source** (using citation format above)
7. **Rate reliability** (‚≠ê scale)
8. **Note any conflicts** (who benefits from this claim?)
9. **Archive source** (PDF, screenshot, archive.org link)

### If Primary Source Not Available:
- **Option A:** Flag as "UNVERIFIED - Awaiting primary source"
- **Option B:** Note secondary source with caveat
- **Never:** Present as fact without noting limitation
