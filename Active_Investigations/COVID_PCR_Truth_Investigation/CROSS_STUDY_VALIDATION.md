We now have **two independent sources** reaching **identical conclusions** about PCR testing limitations:

- **Type:** Meta-analysis of 29 studies globally
- **Institution:** Oxford University (Centre for Evidence-Based Medicine)
- **Date:** September 29, 2020
- **Status:** Preprint (medRxiv)

### Study 2: Singanayagam et al. (Public Health England)
- **Type:** Primary research, 324 samples
- **Institution:** Public Health England (UK government agency)
- **Date:** August 13, 2020 (published BEFORE Jefferson v4)
- **Status:** Peer-reviewed (Eurosurveillance journal)

---

## FINDING-BY-FINDING COMPARISON

### 1. Ct Values Correlate with Infectivity

**Jefferson Review:**
> "Twelve studies reported that Ct values were significantly lower and log copies higher in specimens producing live virus culture."

**PHE Study:**
> "We observed a strong relationship between Ct value and ability to recover infectious virus."

**Match:** ✅ IDENTICAL

---

### 2. The 33% Rule

**Jefferson Review:**
> "Two studies reported the odds of live virus culture reduced by approximately 33% for every one unit increase in Ct."

**PHE Study:**
> "The estimated OR of recovering infectious virus decreased by 0.67 for each unit increase in Ct value (95% CI: 0.58–0.77)."

**Math:** OR of 0.67 = 33% reduction per unit

**Match:** ✅ EXACT NUMERICAL AGREEMENT

---

### 3. High Ct Values = Non-Infectious

**Jefferson Review:**
> "A cut-off RT-PCR Ct > 30 was associated with non-infectious samples."

**PHE Study:**
> "Probability of recovery of virus from samples with Ct > 35 was 8.3% (95% CI: 2.8%–18.4%)."

**Match:** ✅ CONSISTENT (PHE used higher threshold but still very low probability)

---

### 4. Infectivity Declines After Day 8

**Jefferson Review:**
> "Infectivity declines after day 8 even among cases with ongoing high viral loads."

**PHE Study:**
- Week 1 culture positivity: 74%
- Week 2 culture positivity: 20%
- Day 10: 6% probability

**Match:** ✅ IDENTICAL TIMELINE

---

### 5. RNA Shedding vs Infectious Period

**Jefferson Review:**
> "Duration of RNA shedding detected by PCR was far longer compared to detection of live culture. Six out of eight studies reported RNA shedding for longer than 14 days. Yet, infectivity declines after day 8."

**PHE Study:**
> "Median duration of virus shedding as measured by culture was 4 days (IQR: 1–8)."

**Match:** ✅ PCR detects for weeks, culture for days

---

### 6. Asymptomatic Transmission Possible

**Jefferson Review:**
- Noted asymptomatic cases can have detectable virus

**PHE Study:**
> "One of the first reports of virus isolation from cases who remain completely asymptomatic... asymptomatic and presymptomatic persons do represent a source of potentially transmissible virus."

**Match:** ✅ Both confirm asymptomatic transmission
